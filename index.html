About Me

A Little About Myself

I grew up in New Hampshire, where I lived in Bridgewater, Holderness, and most recently, Hanover. In high school, I was very into music as well as sports - I ran track and played football as well as singing in the show choir and leading the male a capella group. In college, I joined the Northeastern track team as a high jumper and helped contribute as a scorer to two conference championships. Academically, Northeastern has allowed me the opportunity to explore the options to available to me within the city, and with the amount of talent and smart minds around, there is never a shortage.

What I'm most excited about right now is the economics half of my degree catching up to the data science half. Most of my work so far has been in research computing or small-scale applied contexts, and I'm genuinely curious about what these skills look like when the stakes are larger and the decisions are business ones. I'm currently seeking co-ops for the Fall '26 cycle (July through December 2026), and the projects I find most interesting are the ones where better data infrastructure leads directly to better decisions, whether in supply chain, finance, operations, or wherever that logic applies.
